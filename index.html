Bora Bora is a stunning island group in the South Pacific, part of the Society Islands of French Polynesia. Known as the "Pearl of the Pacific," it's famous for its turquoise lagoon, white sandy beaches, and luxurious overwater bungalows2. The island is surrounded by a barrier reef and features the remnants of an extinct volcano, with Mount Otemanu as its highest peak

The Phi Phi Islands are a breathtaking archipelago in Thailand, located in the Andaman Sea between Phuket and Krabi. They are part of the Krabi Province and consist of six islands, with the two main ones being Phi Phi Don and Phi Phi Leh. Phi Phi Don is the largest and the only inhabited island, offering accommodations, restaurants, and lively nightlife. Phi Phi Leh, on the other hand, is uninhabited but famous for its stunning Maya Bay, which gained worldwide fame from the movie The Beach

The Maldives is a tropical paradise located in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It's an archipelago of 26 atolls, comprising over 1,000 coral islands. Known for its crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and luxurious overwater bungalows, the Maldives is a dream destination for relaxation and adventure
